About Me

Hello, my name is Matthew Fitzgerald. I am currently in my final semester at Weber State University where I will graduate with a bachelors degree in Full Stack Web Development as well as a certificate of User Experience Design. I have experience working in an IT environment from an internship that I completed over the summer of 2022 at an international company called Management and Training Corporation (MTC) as well as two years of leadership experience from my supervising position at Lagoon Amusement Park.

I am fueled by a passion to create usable and accessible web pages, and always strive to think in the mindset of the user when creating websites and applications because I believe that having a usable website or application that is accessible to everyone should one of the top priorities of any web developer.

My most recent professional success came from the internship at MTC where my main task was to clean up the companies Asset Tracker System (ATS) for all their printers and copiers. This consisted of normalizing names, filling out empty information fields, and making sure that serial numbers were correct for hundreds of devices. By the end of my two month internship almost the entire ATS system had been fixed to meet the standards that the company was looking for, and there was even time for me to move onto working on even more things like getting printer page counts, normalizing laptop and desktop data in ATS, and helping create budgets for MTC’s sites.

Along with that I was also selected into the Lagoon E.L.I.T.E. program. E.L.I.T.E. stands for Excellent in Leadership Improving Through Education, and it is a leadership program for the best of the best management personnel at Lagoon. It is a very selective program that only has three to four people selected from each department each season, and it is built to make the best leaders at Lagoon even better for whatever job they might end up in later on in life, Lagoon or not.

I am currently looking to begin my professional web career after I graduate this April. I am always interested on working on challenges that will help me continue to learn and expand my web development knowledge.
